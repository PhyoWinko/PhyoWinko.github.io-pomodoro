Pomodo Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s.
In Pomodoro, you do focused work during 25-minute intervals — known as pomodoros — and take a five-minute break.
It aims to improve productivity by breaking work into intervals, typically 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

Fun fact

"Pomodoro" means "tomato" in Italian.
The Pomodoro Technique got its name because Francesco Cirillo, its inventor, used a red, tomato-shaped kitchen timer to time his study sessions.
